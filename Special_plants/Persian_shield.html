Persian shield

About the plant

Strobilanthes dyeriana, commonly known as Persian Shield, is a striking and colorful perennial plant admired for its vibrant foliage. Native to Myanmar (Burma), this plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is cultivated for its ornamental characteristics.
The most notable feature of Strobilanthes dyeriana is its large, lance-shaped leaves that display a stunning combination of metallic purple, silver, and green hues. The iridescent quality of the foliage gives the plant a captivating and almost otherworldly appearance. Persian Shield typically forms a bushy, upright growth habit and may reach a height of around 3 to 4 feet. In addition to its eye-catching foliage, it produces slender spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers, adding to its overall charm. This tropical plant thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often grown as an annual in cooler climates. It prefers well-drained soil and benefits from partial shade to full sun exposure. Persian Shield is commonly used in gardens, landscapes, and as a container plant, providing a bold and colorful accent. Due to its vibrant colors and unique foliage, Strobilanthes dyeriana is a popular choice for adding a touch of exotic flair to gardens and tropical-themed landscapes. While it is generally grown for its visual appeal, it is important to note that all parts of the plant may cause mild skin irritation, so handling with care is advisable.
