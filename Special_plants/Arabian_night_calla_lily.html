About the plant

Zantedeschia aethiopica, commonly known as calla lily or arum lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa. Here's some information about Zantedeschia aethiopica: Calla lilies are known for their distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers and large, arrow-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white, cream, or pale yellow and are often mistaken for the actual flower, which is a modified leaf called a spathe. The true flowers are tiny and are found on the central spike (spadix) inside the spathe.
These plants are popular in gardens and floral arrangements due to their elegant and sculptural appearance. Calla lilies are often used in weddings and other special occasions, symbolizing purity and beauty. Calla lilies prefer moist, well-drained soil and can be grown in full sun to partial shade. They are suitable for borders, containers, or as cut flowers. In colder climates, calla lilies are often grown as annuals or lifted and stored indoors during the winter. While calla lilies are generally non-toxic to humans, it's important to note that all parts of the plant contain calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause skin irritation and mild toxicity if ingested. Therefore, care should be taken when handling the plants, and they should be kept out of reach of children and pets. Zantedeschia aethiopica is just one of many calla lily species and hybrids. Various cultivars are available, offering a range of flower colors, including shades of pink, purple, and orange.
