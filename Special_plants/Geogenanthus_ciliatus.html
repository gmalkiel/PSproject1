Geogenanthus ciliatus

About the plant

Geogenanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the Blue Eyes or Blue African Ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family. Native to Central and South America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama, this plant is appreciated for its unique foliage and attractive flowers.
The Blue Eyes plant features long, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green color and finely fringed edges, giving it a delicate appearance. In addition to the foliage, it produces small, three-petaled flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. This plant prefers warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental for its aesthetic appeal. Geogenanthus ciliatus is suitable for shaded to partially shaded garden areas with well-drained soil.
