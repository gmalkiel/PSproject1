About the plant

Colocasia, commonly known as Elephant Ear or Taro, encompasses a group of tropical plants in the Araceae family. These large-leaved plants are prized for their bold, heart-shaped foliage and are native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.
Colocasia esculenta is one of the most well-known species and is cultivated for its edible corms, known as taro roots, which are a staple in many cuisines. The leaves of Colocasia can vary in size and color, ranging from green to deep purples and blacks, depending on the cultivar. These plants thrive in warm, humid climates and are often grown for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Colocasia can be cultivated in moist, well-drained soils and is suitable for water gardens or areas with consistent moisture. Due to their dramatic foliage, Elephant Ears are popular for creating a tropical and exotic atmosphere in gardens, and they can be overwintered indoors in cooler climates.
