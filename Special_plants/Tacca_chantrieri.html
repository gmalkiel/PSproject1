Tacca chantrieri

About the plant

Tacca chantrieri, commonly known as the Black Bat Flower, is an exotic and intriguing flowering plant belonging to the Dioscoreaceae family. Native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia, this perennial plant is renowned for its unique and somewhat eerie appearance. The Black Bat Flower gets its name from the distinctive bracts that resemble bat wings and the long, filamentous extensions that mimic a bat's whiskers.
The plant produces large, dark purple to black flowers that are nearly hidden by the bracts, adding to its mysterious and captivating allure. While the flowers themselves are not as prominent as the bracts, their dark coloration contributes to the overall dramatic effect. Black Bat Flowers thrive in warm, tropical climates and prefer shaded or partially shaded areas with well-drained soil. They are typically grown as ornamental plants in gardens or as potted specimens. Due to their unique appearance and unusual floral structures, Black Bat Flowers are sought after by plant enthusiasts and collectors, adding a touch of exotic elegance to botanical displays.
