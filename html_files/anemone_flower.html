ANEMONE

The anemone flower was declared the official flower of Israel in 2013, so we set out to investigate what is special about this flower

So who are you an anemone?

Latin/scientific name:

Anemone Coronary.

Color:

Very varied, although red is the most common.

Flowering time:

December - April.

Distribution:

Very common in Israel, on the shores of the Galilee, Samaria, the Lowlands,
the Upper Galilee, the Lower Galilee, the Jezreel Valley, the Judean Mountains, the northern Negev,
the Negev Mountain, the Hula, the Beit Shean Valley, the Judean Desert, the Hermon, the Golan.

Characteristics:

a perennial plant with a tuber, from the Nurit family. Besides red, there are plenty of shades of petals - white, pink, blue and purple.

General:

species protected by law. Lasting for several weeks, the petals open in the morning and close
in the evening. In Israel, it is a single species. The anemone was severely damaged in Israel by massive
harvesting and almost became extinct in the 1950s. The disappearance of the anemone and other flowers
was the motive for the acceleration of the legal and educational actions for the conservation of wildflowers,
and indeed these actions were quite successful.

Interesting details:
