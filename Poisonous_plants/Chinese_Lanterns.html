Chinese Lanterns

About the plant

Alkekengi, scientifically known as Physalis alkekengi, refers to the genus of flowering plants that includes the popular ornamental plant commonly known as Chinese lantern or bladder cherry. This plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is appreciated for its distinctive lantern-like husk-covered fruit. Here's some information about Alkekengi: Alkekengi plants are herbaceous perennials that produce sprawling rhizomes. The most notable feature is the papery husk that encases the fruit, resembling a lantern. These husks start off green and change color as they mature, eventually turning shades of orange or red in the fall. The fruit inside the husk is a small berry.
Chinese lanterns, or Physalis alkekengi, are cultivated for decorative purposes. The lanterns are often used in autumn and winter floral arrangements and are prized for their unique appearance. The dried lanterns can be used for long-lasting ornamental displays. In terms of cultivation, Alkekengi plants prefer well-drained soil and thrive in partial to full sunlight. They are relatively low-maintenance and can be propagated through division or by sowing seeds. While Chinese lanterns are generally non-toxic, it's crucial to note that they are primarily grown for ornamental purposes, and their fruit is not intended for consumption. It's important to exercise caution when handling any plant material, and the dried lanterns should be kept out of reach of children and pets. Additionally, in some regions, Chinese lanterns can spread vigorously and become invasive, so it's advisable to manage their growth in garden settings to prevent them from taking over other plants.
