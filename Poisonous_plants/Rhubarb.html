About the plant

Salvia microphylla, commonly known as the Baby Sage or Blackcurrant Sage, is a flowering perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae. Native to Mexico and the American Southwest, this plant is popular for its ornamental value, aromatic foliage, and attractive flowers.
Characterized by small, ovate to lance-shaped leaves, Salvia microphylla exhibits a compact and bushy growth habit. The leaves are often aromatic when crushed, emitting a pleasant fragrance. The plant produces tubular flowers in various shades, including red, pink, purple, or white, depending on the cultivar. The flowers are arranged in whorls along upright stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Salvia microphylla is well-suited for gardens and landscapes, particularly in regions with a warm or Mediterranean climate. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade. This sage species is relatively low-maintenance, and regular deadheading can promote continuous blooming. Cultivars of Salvia microphylla may vary in size, flower color, and growth habits, providing gardeners with a range of options for landscape design. With its vibrant blooms, aromatic foliage, and adaptability, Salvia microphylla is a popular choice for adding color and texture to gardens, borders, and container plantings.
