Lantana

About the plant

Lantana is a genus of flowering plants that includes both shrubs and perennial herbs. These plants are popular in gardens and landscapes due to their colorful and long-lasting flower clusters. Here's some information about Lantana: Lantana plants are known for their clusters of small, tubular flowers that are arranged in spherical umbels. The flowers often change color as they mature, creating an attractive multi-colored effect within the flower cluster. Common flower colors include shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink. Lantana species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and Africa. They thrive in warm climates and are known for their ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions. One popular species is Lantana camara, which is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant. Lantana varieties are available in different sizes, from low-growing ground covers to larger shrubs. The plants are often valued for their ability to attract butterflies with their nectar-rich flowers. While Lantana is beloved for its ornamental qualities, it's important to note that some species may be invasive in certain regions. In areas where Lantana is not native, it can spread rapidly and outcompete native vegetation. Lantana plants are relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in full sun or partial shade. They are drought-tolerant once established and are suitable for garden borders, containers, or hanging baskets. It's worth mentioning that all parts of the Lantana plant are considered toxic if ingested, and contact with the foliage may cause skin irritation in some individuals.
