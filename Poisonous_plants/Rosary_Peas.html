Rosary Peas

About the plant

Abrus precatorius, commonly known as the rosary pea or jequirity bean, is a plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia. It is a slender, twining vine with pinnate leaves, and its distinctive seeds are often used in jewelry and ornamental crafts. The seeds are small and brightly colored, typically red with a black spot, making them visually attractive. However, they contain a toxic compound called abrin.
Abrin is similar to ricin, a toxin found in castor beans, and it can be extremely poisonous. Ingesting or even chewing the seeds can lead to severe poisoning, as abrin inhibits protein synthesis within cells. While the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for various purposes, the potential for toxicity makes it highly dangerous. Handling the seeds without proper care can also pose a risk, as abrin can be absorbed through the skin. Therefore, caution is essential when working with any part of the Abrus precatorius plant. The use of its seeds in jewelry and ornaments raises concerns about accidental ingestion or contact, so it's important to be aware of the potential dangers associated with this plant.
